🌍 What is the International Day of Yoga?

The United Nations declared June 21st as the International Day of Yoga in December 2014, following a proposal by India's Prime Minister Narendra Modi. The first IDY was celebrated on June 21, 2015, with participation from over 190 countries — the world's largest yoga session.

🗓 Every 21 June 🌐 190+ countries 🇺🇳 UN declared

🇮🇳 India & the Yoga Tradition

Yoga originated in ancient India over 5,000 years ago. It is one of India's greatest gifts to the world — a holistic practice that unites body, mind, and spirit. The word "yoga" comes from the Sanskrit root "yuj" meaning to yoke or unite.

2027 Theme

The 13th IDY theme is "Yoga for Harmony and Peace" — reflecting yoga's power to bridge differences, foster inner calm, and build community across cultures.

🧘 What is Yoga?

Yoga is a 5,000-year-old physical, mental, and spiritual practice that originated in India. It combines physical postures (asanas), breathing techniques (pranayama), and meditation (dhyana) to promote wellbeing, flexibility, strength, and inner peace.

Yoga is for everyone — regardless of age, fitness level, or experience. The IDY session is designed to be accessible for all participants, including beginners.

🌿 The 8 Limbs of Yoga (Ashtanga)

1. Yama — Ethics
Non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, continence, non-greed
2. Niyama — Self-discipline
Purity, contentment, austerity, self-study, surrender to the divine
3. Asana — Postures
Physical poses that build strength, flexibility, and balance
4. Pranayama — Breath Control
Regulation of breath to influence the flow of prana (life force)
5. Pratyahara — Sense Withdrawal
Turning attention inward, away from external distractions
6. Dharana — Concentration
Focused attention on a single point or object
7. Dhyana — Meditation
Sustained, uninterrupted awareness
8. Samadhi — Enlightenment
State of blissful union with universal consciousness

💪 Benefits of Yoga

Physical

  • Improves flexibility and range of motion
  • Builds muscle strength and tones the body
  • Improves posture and body awareness
  • Boosts immunity and cardiovascular health
  • Aids digestion and detoxification

Mental & Emotional

  • Reduces stress, anxiety, and depression
  • Improves focus, memory, and sleep quality
  • Cultivates mindfulness and emotional resilience
  • Promotes a sense of calm and inner peace

📜 A Brief History of Yoga

~3000 BCE — Indus Valley Civilization
Earliest evidence of yoga practice found in seals and figurines from the Indus Valley, depicting figures in meditative poses
1500–500 BCE — Vedic Period
The Vedas (Rigveda, Upanishads) introduced early yogic concepts — sacrifices, rituals, and the connection between individual and universal consciousness
500 BCE — Bhagavad Gita
Lord Krishna's discourse to Arjuna established karma yoga, jnana yoga, and bhakti yoga as paths to liberation
~200 BCE — Patanjali's Yoga Sutras
Sage Patanjali codified yoga philosophy into 196 sutras — the definitive text on classical yoga and the eight-limbed path (Ashtanga)
Late 19th century — Yoga Reaches the West
Swami Vivekananda introduced yoga to the West at the Parliament of World Religions in Chicago (1893). Yogis like Krishnamacharya, Iyengar, and Pattabhi Jois later modernised asana practice
2014 — UN Resolution
The UN General Assembly declared June 21 as International Day of Yoga, with a record 177 co-sponsoring nations — the largest number for any UN resolution
2015–Present — Global Celebration
IDY is now celebrated in 190+ countries. India holds mass events at iconic landmarks. Bangkok has hosted thousands of participants each year

✅ Yoga Session Do's & Don'ts

✅ DO

  • Wear loose, comfortable clothing
  • Arrive at least 15 min early
  • Bring your QR code (phone or printed)
  • Bring a water bottle (min 500ml)
  • Practice on the provided mat
  • Listen to the instructor's guidance
  • Breathe naturally and stay hydrated
  • Inform marshals of any injuries

🚫 DON'T

  • Eat a heavy meal within 2 hours before
  • Wear tight or restrictive clothes
  • Push through pain or discomfort
  • Bring valuables — limited storage
  • Use your phone during the session
  • Collect T-shirts on behalf of others
  • Leave mats after the session
  • Bring pets to the venue

👗 What to Wear

Wear your IDY T-shirt (collected at entry) or any light, breathable sportswear. Avoid jeans, belts, or restrictive clothing. Bare feet or yoga socks are recommended for the mat.

🍎 Food & Hydration

  • Do not eat a heavy meal 1–2 hours before the session
  • Light snacks (fruit, nuts) are fine 30–60 minutes before
  • Stay well-hydrated throughout the day
  • Bring your own refillable water bottle — refill stations on site
  • Bangkok in June is hot and humid — drink water before, during, and after

🏥 Health Precautions

Yoga is suitable for all fitness levels. However, if you have any of the following conditions, please inform our on-site marshals:

  • Recent surgery or injury
  • Pregnancy (especially 2nd or 3rd trimester)
  • Severe back or joint conditions
  • High blood pressure or heart conditions

First aid will be available on site. You are not required to perform any pose you are uncomfortable with.
